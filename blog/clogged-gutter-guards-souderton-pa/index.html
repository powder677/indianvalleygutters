Structural Restoration

Stop Re-Nailing Your Gutters.
Upgrade the Hardware.

Spikes work loose. Cheap screws rust. We use heavy-duty rafter mounts that lock your gutters to the house frame, not just the fascia board.

If you can see the nail heads, your gutter is already failing.

The biggest problem in Souderton and Harleysville isn't the rain—it's the freeze-thaw cycle. When water freezes in your gutter, it expands. This pushes the cheap aluminum spikes out of the wood, a process engineers call "Screw-Creep."

Once "Screw-Creep" starts, hammering the nail back in won't fix it. The wood is stripped. You need a structural upgrade.

The "Indian Valley" Repair Standard

1. The 316-Stainless Upgrade

We remove the rusty spikes and install Heavy-Duty Hidden Hangers. We secure them with 316-Marine Grade Stainless Steel screws. They don't rust, they don't snap, and they don't creep.

2. Rafter-Mount Anchoring

If your fascia board is rotting, we don't just attach to it. We use long-shank anchors to bite into the structural rafter tails of your roof. That’s how you get a gutter that can hold a ladder.
