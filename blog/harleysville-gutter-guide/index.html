Valley Convergence Points

Properties near Lederach Golf Club feature deep valleys that funnel massive hydraulic loads into short gutter sections. These "High-Flow Zones" require heavy-duty hidden hangers spaced every 18 inches to prevent the aluminum from warping under the weight of spring downpours.

Underground System Management

Most Harleysville subdivisions utilize buried corrugated lines to manage runoff. Our technical audits in the 19438 zip code frequently find:

  • Root Intrusion: Mature foundation yews and rhododendrons piercing underground lines.
  • Shingle-Grit Blockages: Fine silt from aging 30-year shingles settling in elbow joints.
  • Line Crushing: Heavy Montgomery County clay soil heave compromising thin-walled pipes.

The Bradford Pear & Maple Factor

Planned landscaping in Harleysville developments often features Bradford Pears and Ornamental Maples. Unlike oak leaves, these small, dense leaves create a "matting effect" that creates a waterproof seal over standard mesh guards. We utilize 3,000 PSI hydro-jetting to ensure these organic mats are completely emulsified and flushed from the system.
