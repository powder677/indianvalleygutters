Technical Definition of Soft-Washing

In neighborhoods like Lansdale and Souderton, black streaks on roofs are frequently misidentified as dirt. They are actually a biological infestation of Gloeocapsa Magma. Soft-washing is a technical application of biodegradable surfactants that emulsify these organisms at the root level using **less than 60 PSI**‚Äîroughly the same pressure as a standard garden hose.

The Mechanics of Pressure Damage

  • Granular Depletion: High-PSI water dislodges the limestone-coated granules that provide UV protection. Once these are gone, the asphalt substrate becomes brittle and cracks.
  • Lip Lifting: Pressure directed upward can lift the shingle tab, forcing water into the plywood decking and rafter tails, causing silent structural rot.
  • Warranty Revocation: Most major manufacturers (GAF, Owens Corning) explicitly state that high-pressure washing voids the structural shingle warranty.

The ARMA Standard

The Asphalt Roofing Manufacturers Association (ARMA) recommends only one method for cleaning: a non-pressure chemical application. Our technical restoration protocol follows these exact standards:

  1. Saturation: We apply a specialized sodium hypochlorite-based solution with proprietary surfactants that "stick" to steep Pennsylvania rooflines.
  2. Dwell Time: The solution is allowed to dwell, neutralizing the root systems of moss, lichen, and algae without mechanical force.
  3. Granule Audit: Post-wash, we perform a 12-point inspection of your hidden hangers and fascia joints to ensure the biological growth hasn't compromised the structural rafter tails.
